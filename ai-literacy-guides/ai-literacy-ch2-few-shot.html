Lecture 9 Dr. Mahyar Sharif Vaghefi UT Arlington

2.11 — The Problem: When Words Aren’t Enough

Sometimes the content AI produces is right, but the tone is wrong. Too formal, too generic, or it just doesn’t sound like you. You try to describe what you want, but it’s hard to put into words.

Phrases That Don’t Work

“Make it more engaging” “Sound more professorial” “Be more conversational” “Match my style”

These are like telling a chef “make it taste better” — technically feedback, practically meaningless.

The Solution: Show, Don’t Tell

Instead of describing what you want, show examples. AI pattern-matches to your examples instead of guessing from vague descriptions. Much more precise.

2.12 — The Few-Shot Pattern

Few-shot prompting means giving AI 1–3 examples of good output, then asking for more “like these.” It’s absurdly effective.

The Basic Pattern

Your Examples
(1–3 good ones)
→
AI Pattern-Matches
(tone, length, style)
→
Similar Output
(matches your style)

The Steps:

1. Give context about the task → 2. Provide 1–3 examples of what you want → 3. Say “Create [number] more like these” → 4. Watch the magic happen

What AI Matches From Your Examples

Length Tone Complexity Sentence structure Format Vocabulary level Question style

You’re not describing “your style” — you’re showing your style.

When Should You Use Few-Shot Prompting?

Situation Why Few-Shot Works Better
Specific format or template Examples show the exact structure, so AI reliably copies the pattern.
Particular tone or voice Sample texts teach AI the desired style more clearly than “be professional but friendly.”
Subtle rubrics or grading levels Graded examples demonstrate how you apply Excellent / Good / Needs Improvement.
Generating many similar items “More like these” examples help AI create matching questions with similar difficulty and style.
Vague tasks with multiple interpretations Input–output examples clarify what you really mean.
Structured data from messy text Example conversions teach AI the target fields and format.
Custom teaching or course style A few of your own samples act as “mini-training” without formal model training.

2.13 — Practical Examples for Faculty

Discussion Questions

You have: 3 discussion questions that generate great student engagement (refined over years).

Few-shot prompt: “Here are three discussion questions that work well in my class: [paste examples]. Create 5 more in this style.”

Result: 5 new questions matching the tone, complexity, and engagement style of your proven winners.

Exam Questions

You have: Your best multiple-choice questions from 15 years of exams.

Few-shot prompt: “These are examples of strong exam questions from my finance course: [paste 2–3 examples]. Generate 10 more questions at this level.”

Result: 10 new questions matching the difficulty, format, and analytical depth of your best questions.

Case Study Questions

You have: 3 case study questions that always spark great discussions.

Few-shot prompt: “Here are case study discussion questions that work well: [paste examples]. Create 7 more following this pattern for [new case topic].”

Result: 7 new case questions with the same structure and depth — finally more than just 3!

Feedback Comments

You have: Examples of constructive feedback you’ve given students.

Few-shot prompt: “Here’s how I typically give feedback on student papers: [paste 2–3 examples]. Write feedback for this student’s work in my style: [paste student work].”

Result: Feedback that sounds like you wrote it, maintaining your supportive-but-rigorous voice.

2.14 — Critical Rules for Few-Shot Prompting

Quick Rule of Thumb

Don’t create examples from scratch just for this! The power of few-shot is using examples you ALREADY HAVE. If you don’t have good examples, it’s actually faster to just describe what you want in detail.

Only Show the Great Ones!

Q: What happens if I show AI my bad examples too?
A: AI will average them together. You’ll get “aggressively medium” questions.

Curate your examples. Quality in = quality out.

Few-Shot Is For Replication, Not Creation

✓ USE Few-Shot When: “I have this thing that works — make more.”
✗ SKIP Few-Shot When: You’re creating something new from scratch with no existing examples.

Key Takeaways

✓Show, don’t tell: Examples communicate style better than descriptions like “make it engaging.”
✓The pattern: 1–3 examples + “create more like these” = AI matches length, tone, complexity, and style.
✓Use existing examples: Don’t create examples from scratch — use what you already have that works.
✓Quality matters: Only show your best examples — AI averages what you give it.
✓Replication, not creation: Few-shot is for “make more of this good thing,” not starting from scratch.

“Show me those three great questions, and I’ll give you ten more.”
