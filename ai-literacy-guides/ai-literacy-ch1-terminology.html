1.5 — Speaking the Same Language: AI Terminology

“AI,” “machine learning,” “deep learning,” “generative AI” — are these the same thing? No. Think of them like Russian nesting dolls, each one fitting inside the other.

The Nesting Dolls of AI

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Machine Learning

Deep Learning

Generative AI

(ChatGPT, Claude, DALL·E)

AI Agents

Newest

(Autonomous task execution)

Each layer fits inside the one above it — like Russian nesting dolls.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Definition: Technology that lets machines solve problems in ways similar to humans.

Examples: Spam filters, GPS navigation, fraud detection, recommendation engines, smart home devices

Key Point: AI isn’t just robots — it’s already in your everyday tools!

Machine Learning (ML)

Definition: Teaching computers to find patterns in data without programming every single rule.

Examples: Stock price prediction, customer churn analysis, sales forecasting

Analogy: Like teaching a child to recognize cats by showing hundreds of cat pictures, rather than writing down all the rules that make something a “cat.”

Deep Learning (DL)

Definition: ML with multiple “layers” of analysis, similar to how human brains process information.

Examples: Facial recognition, voice assistants (Siri, Alexa), autonomous vehicles

Analogy: Like recognizing a face — first layer detects edges, second layer identifies eyes/nose/mouth, third layer recognizes whose face it is.

Generative AI

Definition: AI that creates new content — text, images, videos, code — not just analyze existing data.

Examples: ChatGPT (text), DALL·E (images), GitHub Copilot (code), Synthesia (video)

The Breakthrough: AI moved from only identifying cats in photos → to creating images of cats on demand!

AI Agents Newest

Definition: AI that can take actions — browsing the web, executing code, completing multi-step tasks autonomously.

Examples: AutoGPT, AgentGPT, specialized enterprise agents

Key Distinction: If Generative AI is a smart assistant that answers when you ask, AI Agents are smart assistants that can actually go do things for you.

Generative AI vs. AI Agents: Key Differences

Dimension Generative AI AI Agents
Interaction Reactive — responds to prompts Proactive — initiates actions, plans ahead
Task Type Single-step tasks Multi-step, complex workflows
Workflow Human → AI → Human (iterative) Human sets goal → AI executes end-to-end
Human Role High — guides each step Low–Medium — sets goals, reviews outcomes
Analogy Consultant: advises when asked, you implement Executive Assistant: plans and completes tasks end-to-end

1.6 — AI in Your Discipline

No matter what you teach, AI is already in your field. Your students will encounter it professionally. The question isn’t whether — it’s whether they’re ready.

Agents: Real-time market data retrieval

Marketing

Gen AI: Content creation, campaign ideas

Agents: Competitive analysis, trend monitoring

Agents: Regulatory update monitoring

Management

Agents: Industry research

Operations

Information Systems

Key Takeaways

✓AI, ML, Deep Learning, Generative AI, and AI Agents are not the same — they’re nested layers.

✓Generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) is what you’ll mostly encounter in education — AI that creates content.

✓No matter your discipline, AI is already in your field — your students will encounter it professionally.
