Chapter 3, Zone 3: AI for Exam Development

Lecture 20 Dr. Mahyar Sharif Vaghefi UT Arlington

The Exam Creation Problem

Creating a quality exam from scratch takes 8–10 hours — matching difficulty levels, covering all objectives, writing decent distractors for multiple choice. Many faculty end up recycling questions for multiple semesters, knowing students pass them around.

The AI Shift: Writer → Editor

Instead of creating every question from scratch, AI gives you a strong first draft in minutes. You spend your time reviewing and refining — a fundamentally different use of your expertise.

Your Inputs → Fresh Exam Questions

You’ve already done the hardest part — you taught the material. If you followed the session summary workflow, you have everything AI needs to generate a targeted, relevant exam.

1

Session Summaries

Generates questions based on concepts and examples you actually discussed in class — not generic textbook content.

2

Student Questions & Discussions

Reflects the depth and focus of your actual teaching, including topics you spent extra time clarifying.

3

Your Previous Exam

Matches your preferred style, difficulty distribution, and cognitive levels without duplicating a single question.

4

Learning Objectives

Maps questions across Bloom’s taxonomy levels, ensuring comprehensive coverage of all objectives.

The Session Summary Payoff

Those session summaries you created all semester? They now become your exam source material. This is why the session summary workflow compounds — it benefits students during the semester AND makes your exam development faster at the end.

Bloom’s Taxonomy: Six Levels of Thinking

Bloom’s taxonomy is a hierarchy of thinking skills, from simple to complex. Understanding these levels helps you design exams with a deliberate cognitive balance.

6. Create

Combine ideas to produce something new

“Design a pricing strategy for this company…”

5. Evaluate

Make a judgment and defend it

“Which approach is better and why?”

4. Analyze

Break apart and examine relationships

“What factors contributed to this outcome?”

3. Apply

Use the concept in a new situation

“Calculate the NPV given these inputs…”

2. Understand

Explain the concept in your own words

“Explain how supply and demand interact…”

1. Remember

Recall facts and definitions

“Define supply and demand…”

Balance Matters

If all questions are Remember & Understand: you’re only testing surface knowledge. If all questions are Evaluate & Create: you might be testing beyond what you taught. A good exam has a deliberate mix — and AI can tag each question with its Bloom’s level so you see your exam’s cognitive profile at a glance.

What AI Does for You

When generating exam questions, AI can tag each question with its Bloom’s level. You can see at a glance whether your exam is balanced. If you spot gaps, ask AI to generate additional questions at the levels you’re missing.

The Quality Reality Check

How good are AI-generated exam questions? Here’s what the research says.

2025 BMC Medical Education Study

69%

of AI-generated questions were usable with minor edits

31%

had issues: factual errors, misaligned difficulty, poor formatting, or ambiguous wording

The Good News

AI gives you a strong first draft in minutes instead of hours. You spend your time reviewing and refining instead of creating from scratch. That’s a fundamentally different — and better — use of your expertise.

The Reality

Roughly one in three questions needs more than minor tweaking. You still review every single question. Your expertise is the final filter. Always.

You’re shifting from writer to editor.
And you’re a much better editor than AI is a writer — because you know what your students actually need to demonstrate.

Template Prompt: Exam Question Generation

Use this template to generate fresh exam questions based on what you actually taught — not generic textbook content.

Template: Exam Question Generation
Role: You are an assessment expert creating exam questions from my course materials.

Context: Attached are my session summaries and my previous exam for [Course Name]. Learning objectives: [list objectives].

Task: Generate a new exam that:
1. Uses session summaries to ensure questions reflect what was actually taught
2. Matches the previous exam’s style, format, and difficulty distribution
3. Includes [number] MCQs, [number] short-answer, and [number] application questions
4. Varies cognitive levels using Bloom’s taxonomy (tag each question with its level)
5. Has zero overlap with the previous exam
6. Includes an answer key with grading guidance
Constraint: Every question must be answerable only by someone who understood the material — not by someone who memorized the old exam.

What to Attach

• Session summaries from throughout the semester
• Your previous exam (for style and difficulty matching)
• Optionally: Student Q&A captured in session summaries to reflect areas where students needed extra clarification

AI Exam Development — Quick Reference

◆Four Inputs: Session summaries + Student Q&A + Previous exam + Learning objectives
◆Bloom’s Tags: AI labels each question’s cognitive level so you see your exam’s balance
◆Quality: ~69% usable with minor edits; ~31% need more work
◆Your Role: Review every question — you’re the editor, AI is the first drafter

Traditional: 8–10 hours from scratch

With AI: Minutes to generate + focused review and refinement time

“Your expertise is the final filter. Always.”
