Lecture 18 Dr. Mahyar Sharif Vaghefi UT Arlington

What is a Course Chatbot?

A course chatbot is an AI assistant that you configure with your specific course materials — syllabus, lecture notes, assignment guidelines, FAQs, and any other resources. When students interact with it, the chatbot draws from your content to answer questions, explain concepts, and guide them through course material.

Key Distinction

This isn’t generic AI giving random internet answers. It’s an AI that speaks with your voice, understands your course structure, and reinforces what you teach — not what some random source says.

Think of It As…

A teaching assistant that knows your course inside and out, available to students anytime, trained on your materials and your way of explaining things.

Why Build a Course Chatbot?

Course chatbots solve several common teaching challenges:

24/7 Availability

Students get help when they need it, not just during office hours. The 11 PM question gets answered at 11 PM.

Consistent Answers

Every student gets the same accurate information about policies, deadlines, and expectations. No more “but Sarah said you told her something different.”

Personalized Explanations

Students can ask for concepts to be explained differently until they understand — without feeling embarrassed to ask you again.

Reduced Email Load

Routine questions handled automatically so you can focus on complex issues. Significantly fewer 2 AM emails asking “When is the assignment due?”

Available Platforms

You don’t have to build a chatbot from scratch. Several platforms now enable faculty to create custom course chatbots relatively easily.

Custom GPTs (OpenAI)

Build chatbots by uploading documents and writing instructions. Create a custom assistant that draws from your course materials.

✓ Upload syllabus, lecture notes, FAQs

✓ Write custom instructions for behavior

✓ Share link with students

Copilot Studio (Microsoft)

Seamless integration into Microsoft Teams — the environment students already use.

✓ Lives inside existing tools

✓ No separate platform for students to learn

✓ Control over behavior and knowledge boundaries

Important Considerations

Course chatbots are powerful, but there are practical realities to consider:

Catch #1: Licensing Requirements

Tools like Copilot Studio typically require a separate license from your institution. Check with your IT department before investing development time.

Catch #2: Learning Curve

These tools give you significant control over chatbot behavior, knowledge boundaries, and response handling. That’s powerful, but this is not a “set up in an afternoon” project.

Realistic Time Investment

Expect to invest a few hours upfront, then maintain periodically. But once it’s running, it handles hundreds of routine questions without you lifting a finger.

Is a Course Chatbot Right for You?

✓ Likely a Good Fit If…

• You teach large sections with high email volume

• You teach multiple courses and answer similar questions repeatedly

• Students frequently ask about logistics, policies, and deadlines

• Your institution has the necessary platform licenses

✗ May Not Justify Investment If…

• You teach smaller seminars with limited email volume

• Most student questions require nuanced, individual responses

• Your institution lacks the necessary licenses

• You’re not comfortable with periodic maintenance

Quality Assurance: Preventing Wrong Answers

A legitimate concern: What if the chatbot gives wrong answers?

Verify During Setup

Test the chatbot’s responses thoroughly before deploying to students

Review Interactions Periodically

Check student conversations to catch and correct any issues

Set Clear Boundaries

Configure what the chatbot can and can’t answer; direct complex questions to you

A chatbot extends your presence, but it doesn’t replace your judgment.
Complex issues still need human attention.

Course Chatbots — Quick Reference

◆What: AI assistant trained on YOUR course materials, answering with your voice
◆Benefits: 24/7 help, consistent answers, reduced email, personalized explanations
◆Platforms: Custom GPTs (OpenAI), Copilot Studio (Microsoft Teams)
◆Catches: May need separate license; requires upfront time investment
◆First Step: Check with IT about platform availability before investing time

“IT appreciates faculty who do their homework before submitting requests.”

Process Over Product Still Applies

A well-designed course chatbot embodies the “Process Over Product” principle. It doesn’t do students’ work — it helps them learn to do it themselves.

It extends your teaching presence without replacing the human connection that makes education meaningful.

Start Simple

Create a chatbot that answers logistics questions and explains concepts. As you get comfortable, explore more advanced applications.

Your students will appreciate having a knowledgeable resource available whenever they need it — and you’ll appreciate fewer routine emails.
