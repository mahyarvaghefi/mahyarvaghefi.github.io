Chapter 3, Zone 2: Session Summaries

Lecture 17 Dr. Mahyar Sharif Vaghefi UT Arlington

The Overlooked Opportunity

You Already Have Transcripts

Most institutions provide tools like Teams, Zoom, or Panopto that can record sessions and automatically generate transcripts. Your entire lecture, converted to searchable text — probably sitting there unused.

What AI Can Detect from Transcripts

The quality of AI-generated summaries from transcripts is remarkably good. When you explain content verbally — reading formulas aloud, walking through code, describing diagrams — AI recognizes and formats these elements properly.

Formulas & Equations

When you read a formula aloud, AI formats it properly in the summary

Code Snippets

Verbal walkthroughs of syntax appear as actual code, not run-on sentences

Key Takeaways

Things you emphasize, repeat, or circle back to become bolded highlights

The Session Summary Workflow

Four simple steps, about 10 minutes of work per session. Benefits compound all semester.

1

Record Your Session

Use institutional tools (Teams, Zoom, Panopto) to record and auto-generate a transcript. One click when class starts.

2

AI Summarizes

Feed the transcript to AI with a summary prompt requesting structured output with clear sections and key takeaways. Takes a couple minutes.

3

You Verify

Review for accuracy. AI may miss nuances or misinterpret discussion points. Add anything important that was missed. Five-minute review.

4

Share with Students

Post the verified summary to your LMS. Use HTML formatting for clear organization and easy scanning.

Pro Tip: Request HTML Output

Ask AI to generate the summary as HTML instead of plain text. This means formatted text with headers, bullet points, and color-coding. The file uploads directly to Canvas and looks polished and professional instead of like a wall of text. Remember: Students skip things that look like “more reading.”

Triple Benefits of Session Summaries

Three audiences benefit from session summaries — and you’re one of them.

For Students

✓ Reinforces learning after each session

✓ Helps absent students catch up quickly

✓ Creates searchable study reference for exams

For You This Semester

✓ Remember what you covered before planning next class

✓ Ensure continuity — no more “did I already explain that?”

✓ Track connections to upcoming topics

For Future Semesters

✓ Archive of what worked and what fell flat

✓ Questions students asked to address proactively next time

✓ Documentation as you go instead of trying to remember later

Your September self leaves notes for your January self.
By December, you have a complete archive of everything you taught.

Hidden Benefits You Might Not Expect

Engagement Tracking

When summaries live in your LMS, you can track who’s opening them and how often. Which summaries get the most views before exams?

Insight: If nobody opens Week 5 but everyone opens Week 7, that tells you something about what students find valuable — or confusing.

Assessment Source Material

Your summaries become source material for exams and assignments. You have a record of exactly what topics you covered and how deeply.

Benefit: No more testing students on something you only mentioned in passing while thinking you covered it thoroughly. You have receipts.

Template Prompt: Session Summary

Template: Session Summary
Role: You are helping me create a session summary to share with students and keep for my own records.

Context: Here is the transcript from my [Course Name] class on [Date].

Task: Create a clear, organized summary that captures what happened in this session. Include whatever is relevant:
• Key concepts covered
• Examples discussed
• Important questions raised
• Connections to other topics
• Action items for students
• Whatever else emerged from this particular class
Format [Optional]: Use HTML formatting (headers, bullet points, bold) so it displays cleanly.

Constraint: Keep it scannable — students should get the essentials in 3–4 minutes. Adapt the structure to fit what actually happened.

Verification Caveat

AI transcription and summarization can miss context, misattribute comments, or oversimplify nuanced discussions. Transcripts aren’t perfect — especially if audio quality is poor or multiple people talk at once. Your 5 minutes of verification prevents confusion and maintains trust.

Session Summary Workflow

◆Record: Hit record when class starts — one click
◆Summarize: Feed transcript to AI with summary prompt — 2 minutes
◆Verify: Review for accuracy, add what’s missing — 5 minutes
◆Share: Post to LMS with HTML formatting — 1 minute

Total time: ~10 minutes per session

Benefits compound all semester • Complete archive by December
