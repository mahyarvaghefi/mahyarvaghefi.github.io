Lecture 11 Dr. Mahyar Sharif Vaghefi UT Arlington

3.1 — The Foundational Principle

Before diving into practical tools and techniques, there’s one principle that should guide every decision about AI in education. This principle will determine whether AI makes your teaching better or worse.

Process Over Product

AI is most valuable when it enhances the PROCESS of learning — not when it simply delivers the final product.

Important Note

This principle applies equally to students using AI for assignments AND faculty using AI for teaching materials.

3.2 — The Pill vs. Exercise

Imagine you’re slightly overweight and want to get healthier. You have two options:

Option 1: The Pill

✓ Take a pill that helps you lose weight

✓ Minimal effort required

✓ No side effects

✓ You achieve the goal: weight loss

Result: You get thinner. That’s it. You’re the same person, just lighter.

Option 2: Exercise

✓ Commit to regular exercise

✓ Requires consistent effort

✓ Takes time and discipline

✓ You achieve the goal: weight loss

Result: You become a completely different person — with discipline, mental resilience, cardiovascular health, and strength.

The value isn’t in the outcome — it’s in what happens to YOU through the process.

3.3 — Applying This to Education

In education, the final product — the essay, the case analysis, the financial model — is rarely the real goal. These are vehicles for learning, not destinations.

The Learning Transformation

A student who struggles through writing an essay develops critical thinking, clear communication, and deep understanding. They become a different person through that effort.

Using AI to Get Answers Directly…

…is like taking the pill. You get the product. You miss the transformation.

“But Doesn’t That Mean We Should Ban AI Entirely?”

Not at all. The question isn’t whether you use AI. It’s why you use it.

3.4 — This Applies to Both Students AND Faculty

Here’s what most people miss: This principle applies equally to students and faculty — both sides of the classroom.

For Students: AI as Training Partner, Not Answer Machine

✗ AI as “The Pill” — Skipping the Process

“Write my essay on supply chain management”

“Solve this finance problem for me”

“Create a complete marketing plan”

Student gets the product but misses the transformation. They remain unchanged.

✓ AI as “Training Partner” — Enhancing the Process

“Help me understand where my argument is weak”

“Explain this concept in simpler terms”

“What other angles should I consider?”

Student does the work with better guidance. They emerge stronger.

For Faculty: AI to Enrich Learning, Not Just Save Time

✗ AI as “The Pill” — Making YOUR Job Easier

• Generate slides without reviewing them

• Create assignments without thinking about learning objectives

• Accept AI output without adding your expertise

Students will notice and rightly question the value.

✓ AI as “Training Partner” — Making Learning Better

• Generate more practice problems at varied difficulty

• Create richer case studies with multiple angles

• Develop more comprehensive feedback on student work

Students benefit from a more comprehensive learning experience.

The Key Distinction: One approach makes your job easier. The other makes the learning experience better.

3.5 — Reframing the Question

When people find out something involved AI, they often dismiss it: “Oh, that’s just AI-generated.” This dismissive attitude comes from not understanding our principle.

✗ Wrong Question:

“Was AI used?”

✓ Right Question:

“HOW was AI used?”

Evaluating AI Use

Question Good AI Use Looks Like…
Was there clear intent? Used AI strategically with a specific goal, not just “let’s see what AI says”
Was human judgment applied? Evaluated, selected, and refined outputs rather than accepting blindly
Did it enhance the process? AI helped explore options or clarify thinking, not skip the thinking
Is the work truly owned? Can explain, defend, and build upon the work with understanding

3.6 — The Three Zones of AI Integration

With this principle as your foundation, there are three distinct opportunity zones for AI integration in your teaching:

1

Course Preparation

Enhancing materials, updating content, creating resources — all BEFORE class begins.

Syllabus development Course materials Extra materials
2

Content Delivery

Using AI during live sessions to demonstrate concepts, create interactive moments, generate summaries.

Class session summaries Critical thinking activities Current event examples
3

Assessment

Developing assessments, creating rubrics, providing feedback, redesigning assignments for the AI era.

Assessment creation Rubric development Grading assistance Assignment redesign

Key Takeaways

✓Process Over Product — AI is most valuable when it enhances learning, not when it delivers the final product.
✓The Pill vs. Exercise — Using AI to skip work is like taking a pill. The transformation happens through the process.
✓Applies to Everyone — This principle applies equally to students AND faculty.
✓Ask the Right Question — Not “Was AI used?” but “HOW was AI used?”
✓Three Zones — AI can help with Course Preparation, Content Delivery, and Assessment.

“The question isn’t whether you use AI. It’s why you use it.”
