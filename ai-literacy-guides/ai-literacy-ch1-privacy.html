1.12 — The Golden Rule of AI Privacy

Never Upload What You Can’t Make Public

If you wouldn’t post it publicly on social media — don’t put it in AI.

What Happens to Your Data?

1

Data Collection

Your inputs and conversations may be saved on company servers — sometimes indefinitely.

2

Training Use

Your data may be used to train and improve future AI models.

3

Human Review

Employees may review conversations for quality control and safety purposes.

4

Future Outputs

Your input could influence responses to other people — a potential plagiarism nightmare.

The Plagiarism Nightmare

“So if I upload a student paper… it could theoretically appear in someone else’s AI output someday.”

“That’s a plagiarism nightmare I don’t even want to think about.”

1.13 — What You Should NEVER Upload

These items should never be entered into AI tools without enterprise-level privacy protections:

Student Information

Names, grades, personal details — FERPA violation

Proprietary Business Data

Confidential company information, trade secrets

Financial Information

SSNs, credit card numbers, bank details

Medical Records

Protected health information — HIPAA violation

Legal Documents

Contracts, privileged communications

Personal Identifiable Information (PII)

Addresses, phone numbers, personal emails

1.14 — The Data Training Spectrum: Tool Selection Matters

Not all AI tools are equal when it comes to privacy. Think of it as a spectrum from lowest to highest privacy protection.

Free Consumer Tools

Lowest Privacy

Examples:

ChatGPT Free Gemini Free Claude Free

May use your conversations to improve/train models. You’re the product, not the customer.

Not recommended for research or sensitive work

Paid Consumer Tools

Medium Privacy

Examples (~$20/month):

ChatGPT Plus Claude Pro Gemini Advanced

Often have opt-out options, but policies vary. Better than free, but not guaranteed privacy.

Better with opt-out enabled — check settings!

Enterprise / University Tools

Highest Privacy

Examples:

ChatGPT Enterprise Claude for Work Copilot Enterprise University-licensed AI

Contractual guarantees that your data is NOT used for training. True privacy protection.

Safe for research if your institution has this

Verify What Your University Actually Has

“We have AI” and “We have privacy-protected AI” are NOT the same thing.

Ask IT. Read the policy. Then decide what you’re comfortable uploading.

1.16 — Quick Decision Matrix: Which Tool for What?

Use Case Recommended Tool Tier
General concepts, public information Any tool
Student names / grades Enterprise ONLY or NO AI
Unpublished research Enterprise ONLY
Grammar checking (drafts) Paid or Enterprise
Public course materials Any tool

Rule of Thumb

Free tools for playing around, paid tools for medium-sensitivity work, enterprise tools for anything that matters.

Key Takeaways

✗Never upload what you can’t make public — student data, proprietary info, PII.
✗Free tools = lowest privacy — your data may train future models.
⚠Paid tools = medium privacy — check opt-out settings carefully.
✓Enterprise/University tools = highest privacy — contractual data protection.
✓Ask IT what your university actually has — verify the privacy policy.
✓When in doubt — don’t upload it.
